# Objective 4.7 - Communication Techniques and Professionalism

### Professional Appearance

**Definition:** Maintaining a clean and appropriate appearance in the workplace.
**Example:** Wearing clean business attire while supporting office staff.

### Appropriate Attire

**Definition:** Clothing that matches the requirements of the work environment.
**Example:** Wearing safety boots in a warehouse environment.

### Formal Attire

**Definition:** Professional clothing typically required in executive or corporate settings.
**Example:** Wearing a suit and tie during a boardroom meeting.

### Business Casual

**Definition:** Professional but less formal workplace clothing.
**Example:** Wearing a collared shirt and slacks in an office.

---

### Proper Language

**Definition:** Communicating clearly and professionally with customers.
**Example:** Saying "internet connection" instead of technical jargon the customer may not understand.

### Avoid Jargon, Acronyms, and Slang

**Definition:** Using language that customers can easily understand.
**Example:** Saying "hard drive" instead of "SSD" when speaking to a non-technical user.

---

### Positive Attitude / Project Confidence

**Definition:** Remaining professional, calm, and confident when assisting customers.
**Example:** Reassuring a user that you will investigate and resolve the issue.

### Active Listening

**Definition:** Paying full attention to the customer without interrupting.
**Example:** Allowing a user to fully explain a problem before troubleshooting.

### Avoid Interrupting the Customer

**Definition:** Letting the customer finish speaking before responding.
**Example:** Waiting until the user finishes describing an error message.

---

### Cultural Sensitivity

**Definition:** Respecting cultural differences and workplace diversity.
**Example:** Being mindful of different communication styles and customs.

### Professional Titles and Designations

**Definition:** Using appropriate titles when addressing individuals.
**Example:** Addressing a customer as Dr. Smith when appropriate.

---

### Be On Time

**Definition:** Arriving at scheduled appointments or meetings as planned.
**Example:** Arriving at a workstation upgrade appointment five minutes early.

### Contact Customer if Late

**Definition:** Informing the customer when a delay occurs.
**Example:** Calling a user to explain that support will arrive 15 minutes late.

---

### Avoid Distractions

**Definition:** Staying focused on the customer and the task being performed.
**Example:** Ignoring personal notifications while troubleshooting.

### Personal Calls

**Definition:** Personal phone conversations that interfere with work duties.
**Example:** Avoiding personal calls during a support appointment.

### Texting/Social Media Sites

**Definition:** Personal messaging and social media use during work activities.
**Example:** Not checking social media while assisting a customer.

### Personal Interruptions

**Definition:** Non-work-related activities that disrupt customer service.
**Example:** Leaving a support session to discuss personal matters.

---

### Difficult Customers or Situations

**Definition:** Challenging interactions that require professionalism and patience.
**Example:** Assisting an upset customer whose computer has crashed.

### Do Not Argue or Be Defensive

**Definition:** Remaining calm and professional during disagreements.
**Example:** Explaining troubleshooting steps without blaming the customer.

### Avoid Dismissing Customer Issues

**Definition:** Taking customer concerns seriously regardless of their cause.
**Example:** Investigating a reported issue even if it seems minor.

### Avoid Being Judgmental

**Definition:** Treating all customers respectfully and without criticism.
**Example:** Helping a user who accidentally deleted files without making negative comments.

### Clarify Customer Statements

**Definition:** Asking questions to better understand the problem.
**Example:** Asking, "What were you doing when the error occurred?"

### Open-Ended Questions

**Definition:** Questions that encourage detailed responses.
**Example:** "Can you describe what happened before the issue started?"

### Restate the Issue

**Definition:** Repeating the problem in your own words to confirm understanding.
**Example:** "So the printer stops responding after you send a print job?"

### Verify Understanding

**Definition:** Confirming that both parties understand the issue correctly.
**Example:** "Is that an accurate description of the problem?"

### Use Discretion and Professionalism

**Definition:** Handling sensitive information appropriately and professionally.
**Example:** Not discussing customer issues with unauthorized individuals.

---

### Set and Meet Expectations

**Definition:** Providing realistic timelines and commitments.
**Example:** Informing the customer that repairs will take approximately two hours.

### Communicate Status

**Definition:** Keeping customers informed about progress.
**Example:** Sending updates while waiting for replacement parts.

### Offer Repair/Replacement Options

**Definition:** Presenting available solutions to the customer.
**Example:** Offering either a hardware repair or device replacement.

### Provide Proper Documentation

**Definition:** Recording services performed and solutions implemented.
**Example:** Providing a completed service report after repairs.

### Follow Up with Customer/User

**Definition:** Checking that the issue remains resolved after service.
**Example:** Contacting the user the next day to verify satisfaction.

---

### Handle Confidential and Private Materials Appropriately

**Definition:** Protecting customer information from unauthorized access or disclosure.
**Example:** Closing confidential documents before leaving a workstation.

### Information Located on a Computer, Desktop, Printer, etc.

**Definition:** Sensitive customer information found in physical or digital locations.
**Example:** Collecting printed payroll documents left on a printer tray.
