# Objective 4.4 - Common Safety Procedures

### Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) Straps

**Definition:** A wrist strap that prevents static electricity from damaging electronic components.
**Example:** Wearing an ESD strap while installing RAM.

### ESD Mats

**Definition:** A grounded mat that protects components from electrostatic discharge.
**Example:** Placing a motherboard on an ESD mat during repairs.

### Electrical Safety

**Definition:** Procedures used to prevent electrical shock and equipment damage.
**Example:** Turning off power before opening a computer case.

### Equipment Grounding

**Definition:** Connecting equipment to earth ground to safely dissipate electrical current.
**Example:** Using a grounded power outlet for a desktop PC.

### Proper Component Handling and Storage

**Definition:** Safe methods for handling and storing computer components to prevent damage.
**Example:** Holding a circuit board by its edges instead of touching the chips.

### Cable Management

**Definition:** Organizing cables to improve safety, airflow, and maintenance.
**Example:** Using cable ties to bundle network cables.

### Antistatic Bags

**Definition:** Special bags that protect electronic components from static electricity.
**Example:** Storing a graphics card in an antistatic bag.

### Compliance with Government Regulations

**Definition:** Following laws and regulations related to workplace safety and environmental protection.
**Example:** Properly disposing of old batteries according to local regulations.

### Personal Safety

**Definition:** Practices that protect technicians from injury while working.
**Example:** Wearing appropriate protective equipment during repairs.

### Disconnect Power Before Repairing PC

**Definition:** Removing power from a device before performing maintenance or repairs.
**Example:** Unplugging a desktop computer before replacing the power supply.

### Lifting Techniques

**Definition:** Safe methods for lifting heavy equipment to prevent injury.
**Example:** Lifting a server with your legs instead of your back.

### Fire Safety

**Definition:** Procedures used to prevent and respond to fires.
**Example:** Using a Class C fire extinguisher on an electrical fire.

### Safety Goggles

**Definition:** Protective eyewear used to prevent eye injuries.
**Example:** Wearing safety goggles while drilling into a computer rack.

### Air Filter Mask

**Definition:** A mask that protects against dust, particles, and airborne contaminants.
**Example:** Wearing an air filter mask while cleaning dusty server equipment.
