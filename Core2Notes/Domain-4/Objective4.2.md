# Objective 4.2 - Change Management Procedures

# Documented Business Processes

### Rollback Plan

**Definition:** A documented procedure to restore a system to its previous state if a change fails.
**Example:** Reverting a server to its previous configuration after a failed update.

### Backup Plan

**Definition:** A procedure for creating copies of data before making changes.
**Example:** Backing up a database before upgrading software.

### Sandbox Testing

**Definition:** Testing changes in an isolated environment before production deployment.
**Example:** Testing a Windows update in a virtual machine before company-wide deployment.

### Responsible Staff Members

**Definition:** Personnel assigned to plan, approve, implement, or monitor a change.
**Example:** The network administrator responsible for a firewall upgrade.

---

# Change Management

### Request Forms

**Definition:** Documents used to formally request a system change.
**Example:** A form requesting the installation of a new application.

### Purpose of the Change

**Definition:** The reason a change is being made.
**Example:** Upgrading software to fix security vulnerabilities.

### Scope of the Change

**Definition:** The systems, users, or services affected by the change.
**Example:** Updating Microsoft Office on all Finance Department computers.

### Change Type

**Definition:** The classification of a change based on risk and urgency.
**Example:** Installing a routine security update.

---

### Standard Change

**Definition:** A low-risk, pre-approved change performed regularly.
**Example:** Monthly operating system updates.

### Normal Change

**Definition:** A planned change requiring review and approval before implementation.
**Example:** Replacing a company's email server.

### Emergency Change

**Definition:** An urgent change implemented to resolve a critical issue.
**Example:** Applying a security patch during an active cyberattack.

---

### Date and Time of Change

**Definition:** The scheduled time when a change will occur.
**Example:** Server maintenance scheduled for Saturday at 10:00 PM.

### Change Freeze

**Definition:** A period during which changes are not allowed.
**Example:** No system updates during the holiday shopping season.

### Maintenance Windows

**Definition:** Approved time periods for performing system maintenance.
**Example:** Every Sunday from 12:00 AM to 4:00 AM.

---

### Affected Systems/Impact

**Definition:** Identification of systems and users that may be impacted by a change.
**Example:** Email services unavailable during a mail server upgrade.

### Risk Analysis

**Definition:** Evaluation of potential problems that could result from a change.
**Example:** Assessing the possibility of downtime during a network upgrade.

### Risk Level

**Definition:** The degree of risk associated with a change.
**Example:** Upgrading a production database classified as High Risk.

---

### Change Board Approvals

**Definition:** Formal authorization from a change advisory board before implementation.
**Example:** CAB approval required before upgrading critical servers.

### Implementation

**Definition:** The process of carrying out an approved change.
**Example:** Installing and configuring a new firewall.

### Peer Review

**Definition:** Evaluation of a proposed change by another qualified staff member.
**Example:** A second administrator reviewing a firewall rule change.

### End-User Acceptance

**Definition:** Confirmation from users that a change works as expected.
**Example:** Employees verifying that a new payroll application functions correctly.
