# Objective 4.6 - Prohibited Content/Activity, Privacy, Licensing, and Policy Concepts

# Incident Response

### Chain of Custody

**Definition:** A documented record showing who has handled evidence from collection to final disposition.
**Example:** Recording every technician who accessed a confiscated hard drive.

### Informing Management/Law Enforcement as Necessary

**Definition:** Reporting security incidents to appropriate authorities when required.
**Example:** Notifying law enforcement after discovering illegal content on a company computer.

### Copy of Drive (Data Integrity and Preservation)

**Definition:** Creating a forensic copy of a drive to preserve original evidence.
**Example:** Imaging a suspect hard drive before investigation.

### Incident Documentation

**Definition:** Recording all details related to a security incident.
**Example:** Documenting the time, actions taken, and systems affected during a malware outbreak.

### Order of Volatility

**Definition:** The sequence for collecting data from most volatile to least volatile sources.
**Example:** Capturing RAM contents before shutting down a compromised computer.

# Licensing / DRM / EULA

### Valid Licenses

**Definition:** Legally obtained permissions to use software.
**Example:** Purchasing a licensed copy of Microsoft Office.

### Perpetual License Agreement

**Definition:** A license that allows indefinite use of software after purchase.
**Example:** Buying a standalone software package with no expiration date.

### Personal-Use License vs. Corporate-Use License

**Definition:** Different licensing terms for individual users and organizations.
**Example:** A home-use antivirus license cannot be installed on company computers.

### Open-Source License

**Definition:** A license allowing users to view, modify, and distribute source code under specific terms.
**Example:** Using Linux under the GNU General Public License (GPL).

### Digital Rights Management (DRM)

**Definition:** Technology used to control access to copyrighted digital content.
**Example:** A movie purchased online that can only be played through an authorized application.

### End-User License Agreement (EULA)

**Definition:** A legal agreement defining how software may be used.
**Example:** Accepting a software EULA during installation.

---

# Non-Disclosure Agreements

### Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

**Definition:** A legal agreement preventing disclosure of confidential information.
**Example:** An employee signing an NDA before accessing company trade secrets.

### Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (MNDA)

**Definition:** An agreement where both parties agree to protect confidential information.
**Example:** Two companies sharing project details under an MNDA.

---

# Regulated Data

### Credit Card Payment Information

**Definition:** Financial data used to process payment card transactions.
**Example:** A customer's credit card number.

### Personal Government-Issued Information

**Definition:** Information issued by a government for identification purposes.
**Example:** A passport number.

### Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

**Definition:** Information that can identify a specific individual.
**Example:** A person's full name and Social Security number.

### Healthcare Data

**Definition:** Medical information related to an individual's health.
**Example:** A patient's medical record.

### Data Retention Requirements

**Definition:** Rules specifying how long data must be stored before disposal.
**Example:** Retaining financial records for seven years.

---

# Policies and Compliance

### Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

**Definition:** Rules defining appropriate use of company technology resources.
**Example:** Employees may not use company computers for illegal activities.

### Regulatory and Business Compliance Requirements

**Definition:** Legal and organizational requirements that govern business operations and data handling.
**Example:** A company following PCI DSS requirements for payment processing.

### Splash Screens

**Definition:** Login screens displaying security or legal notices before system access.
**Example:** A warning banner stating that system activity is monitored.
