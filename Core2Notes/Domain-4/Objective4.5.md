# Objective 4.5 - Environmental Impacts and Local Environment Controls

### Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS)

**Definition:** A document that provides information on the safe handling, storage, and disposal of hazardous materials.
**Example:** Reviewing an MSDS before cleaning a toner spill.

### Proper Battery Disposal

**Definition:** Disposing of batteries according to environmental and safety regulations.
**Example:** Recycling a lithium-ion laptop battery at an approved collection center.

### Proper Toner Disposal

**Definition:** Disposing of printer toner cartridges in an environmentally safe manner.
**Example:** Returning used toner cartridges to the manufacturer for recycling.

### Proper Disposal of Other Devices and Assets

**Definition:** Disposing of electronic equipment according to environmental regulations.
**Example:** Sending old hard drives to a certified e-waste recycling facility.

---

### Temperature Awareness

**Definition:** Maintaining appropriate temperatures to prevent equipment damage.
**Example:** Keeping a server room at a recommended operating temperature.

### Humidity-Level Awareness

**Definition:** Monitoring humidity to reduce static electricity and moisture-related damage.
**Example:** Maintaining a server room humidity level between 40% and 60%.

### Proper Ventilation

**Definition:** Ensuring adequate airflow around equipment to prevent overheating.
**Example:** Leaving space around servers for proper air circulation.

### Location/Equipment Placement

**Definition:** Positioning equipment in suitable environments for safe operation.
**Example:** Keeping servers away from direct sunlight and heat sources.

### Dust Cleanup

**Definition:** Removing dust to improve airflow and reduce hardware failures.
**Example:** Cleaning dust from computer vents during routine maintenance.

### Compressed Air

**Definition:** A tool used to remove dust from electronic equipment without physical contact.
**Example:** Blowing dust out of a desktop computer's cooling fans.

### Vacuums

**Definition:** Specialized vacuums used to safely remove dust from equipment.
**Example:** Using an ESD-safe vacuum to clean a server rack.

---

### Power Surges

**Definition:** Short increases in voltage that can damage electronic equipment.
**Example:** A lightning strike causing a sudden voltage spike.

### Brownouts

**Definition:** Temporary drops in voltage below normal operating levels.
**Example:** Lights dimming when a building experiences reduced power.

### Blackouts

**Definition:** Complete loss of electrical power.
**Example:** A city-wide power outage during a storm.

### Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS)

**Definition:** A battery-powered device that provides temporary power during outages.
**Example:** A UPS keeping a server running long enough for a safe shutdown.

### Surge Suppressor

**Definition:** A device that protects equipment from voltage spikes.
**Example:** Connecting a workstation to a surge-protected power strip.
