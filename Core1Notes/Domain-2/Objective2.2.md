Objective 2.2 - Wireless Networking Technologies

Frequencies

2.4GHz

Definition: A wireless frequency band that provides longer range but lower speeds than higher-frequency bands.
Example: Connecting a smart home device to a 2.4GHz Wi-Fi network.

5GHz

Definition: A wireless frequency band that provides higher speeds but shorter range than 2.4GHz.
Example: Streaming 4K video over a 5GHz Wi-Fi connection.

6GHz

Definition: A wireless frequency band used by Wi-Fi 6E and Wi-Fi 7 that offers higher speeds and less interference.
Example: Connecting a Wi-Fi 6E laptop to a 6GHz wireless network.

---

Channels

Regulations

Definition: Rules governing the use of wireless frequencies and channels in a region.
Example: Using only approved Wi-Fi channels in a specific country.

Channel Selection

Definition: The process of choosing the best wireless channel to reduce interference.
Example: Changing a router from Channel 6 to Channel 11.

Channel Widths

Definition: The amount of frequency spectrum allocated to a wireless channel.
Example: Configuring an 80MHz channel width for faster Wi-Fi speeds.

Frequencies

Definition: Radio waves used to transmit wireless network data.
Example: A wireless router broadcasting on the 5GHz frequency band.

Bands

Definition: Groups of wireless frequencies used for communication.
Example: A dual-band router supporting both 2.4GHz and 5GHz bands.

---

Wireless Technologies

Bluetooth

Definition: A short-range wireless technology used to connect nearby devices.
Example: Pairing wireless headphones with a smartphone.

802.11 Standards

Definition: IEEE standards that define how Wi-Fi networks operate.
Example: Connecting a laptop to an 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6) network.

Near-Field Communication (NFC)

Definition: A short-range wireless technology used for data exchange and contactless transactions.
Example: Paying for groceries using a smartphone's NFC feature.

Radio-Frequency Identification (RFID)

Definition: A technology that uses radio waves to identify and track tagged objects.
Example: Using an employee access card to unlock a secure door.
