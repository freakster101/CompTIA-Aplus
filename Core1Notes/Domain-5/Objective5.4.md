Objective 5.4 - Troubleshoot Common Mobile Device Issues

Common Symptoms

Poor Battery Health

Definition: Reduced battery capacity causing shorter usage time between charges.
Example: A phone that only lasts a few hours after a full charge.

Swollen Battery

Definition: A battery that has expanded due to internal failure or aging.
Example: A smartphone screen lifting away from the frame because of battery swelling.

Broken Screen

Definition: Physical damage to the display glass or panel.
Example: A cracked smartphone screen after being dropped.

Improper Charging

Definition: A device failing to charge correctly or charging very slowly.
Example: A damaged charging cable causing intermittent charging.

Poor/No Connectivity

Definition: Difficulty connecting to cellular, Wi-Fi, or Bluetooth networks.
Example: A smartphone unable to connect to a Wi-Fi network.

Liquid Damage

Definition: Internal component damage caused by exposure to liquids.
Example: A phone failing to power on after being dropped in water.

Overheating

Definition: Excessive device temperature affecting performance or operation.
Example: A phone becoming very hot while running demanding applications.

Digitizer Issues

Definition: Problems with the touch-sensitive layer of a display.
Example: Parts of a touchscreen not responding to touch input.

Physically Damaged Ports

Definition: Wear or damage to device connectors and ports.
Example: A bent USB-C port preventing proper charging.

Malware

Definition: Malicious software that negatively affects device operation.
Example: A mobile app displaying unwanted advertisements and collecting data.

Cursor Drift/Touch Calibration

Definition: Incorrect touch or pointer response due to calibration issues.
Example: A stylus selecting items slightly away from where it touches the screen.

Unable to Install New Applications

Definition: A condition preventing software installation on the device.
Example: Insufficient storage space blocking app installation.

Stylus Does Not Work

Definition: Failure of a stylus to interact properly with a device.
Example: A dead stylus battery preventing input on a tablet.

Degraded Performance

Definition: Slower-than-normal device operation and responsiveness.
Example: A smartphone lagging due to low available storage.
