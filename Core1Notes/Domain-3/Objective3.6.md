Objective 3.6 - Power Supplies

Input Voltage

110-120 VAC

Definition: A common AC input voltage standard used in countries such as the United States and Canada.
Example: A desktop PC plugged into a 120V wall outlet.

220-240 VAC

Definition: A common AC input voltage standard used in many countries outside North America.
Example: A computer connected to a 230V wall outlet in Europe.

---

Output Voltage

3.3V

Definition: A power supply output voltage used by various motherboard components and circuits.
Example: Supplying power to memory modules and chipsets.

5V

Definition: A power supply output voltage used by storage devices and USB ports.
Example: Providing power to a USB flash drive.

12V

Definition: A power supply output voltage used by high-power components.
Example: Supplying power to a CPU and graphics card.

---

Power Connectors

20+4 Pin Motherboard Connector

Definition: The primary power connector that supplies power to the motherboard.
Example: Connecting a power supply to an ATX motherboard.

---

Power Supply Types

Redundant Power Supply

Definition: A power supply configuration with multiple power units for fault tolerance.
Example: A server continuing to operate after one power supply fails.

Modular Power Supply

Definition: A power supply that allows unused cables to be detached.
Example: Installing only the required power cables in a custom PC build.

---

Power Supply Specifications

Wattage Rating

Definition: The maximum amount of power a power supply can deliver.
Example: Using a 750-watt power supply for a gaming computer.

Energy Efficiency

Definition: A measure of how effectively a power supply converts AC power into DC power.
Example: An 80 PLUS Gold certified power supply reducing energy waste.
