Objective 3.8 - Printer Maintenance

Laser Printers

Laser Printer

Definition: A printer that uses toner, a laser, and a drum to produce printed documents.
Example: Printing office reports with a laser printer.

Replace Toner

Definition: Replacing the toner cartridge when toner levels are low or depleted.
Example: Installing a new black toner cartridge.

Apply Maintenance Kit

Definition: Replacing manufacturer-recommended maintenance components.
Example: Installing a maintenance kit that includes rollers and a fuser.

Calibrate

Definition: Adjusting printer settings to improve print quality and alignment.
Example: Running a color calibration process.

Clean

Definition: Removing toner dust and debris from printer components.
Example: Cleaning the printer's interior during maintenance.

---

Inkjet Printers

Ink Cartridge

Definition: A container that holds liquid ink for printing.
Example: Replacing an empty cyan ink cartridge.

Printhead

Definition: A component that sprays ink onto paper.
Example: Replacing a clogged printhead.

Roller

Definition: A component that feeds paper through the printer.
Example: Cleaning paper-feed rollers to prevent misfeeds.

Feeder

Definition: A mechanism that moves paper into the printer.
Example: Loading paper into the automatic feeder.

Clean Printheads

Definition: A maintenance procedure that removes dried ink from print nozzles.
Example: Running the printer's printhead cleaning utility.

Replace Cartridges

Definition: Installing new ink cartridges when existing cartridges are empty.
Example: Replacing a depleted black ink cartridge.

Calibrate

Definition: Adjusting print alignment and color accuracy.
Example: Performing printhead alignment after cartridge replacement.

Clear Jams

Definition: Removing stuck paper from the printer's paper path.
Example: Carefully removing jammed paper from the feeder.

---

Thermal Printers

Thermal Printer

Definition: A printer that uses heat to create images on special paper.
Example: Printing receipts at a retail store.

Feed Assembly

Definition: The mechanism that advances paper through a thermal printer.
Example: Replacing a worn paper feed assembly.

Special Thermal Paper

Definition: Heat-sensitive paper designed for thermal printers.
Example: Loading a roll of receipt paper into a thermal printer.

Replace Paper

Definition: Installing a new roll or stack of thermal paper.
Example: Replacing an empty receipt paper roll.

Clean Heating Element

Definition: Removing residue from the printhead heating surface.
Example: Cleaning the thermal printhead with approved materials.

Remove Debris

Definition: Clearing dust and paper particles from the printer.
Example: Removing paper fragments from the paper path.

---

Impact Printers

Impact Printer

Definition: A printer that produces output by striking an ink ribbon against paper.
Example: Printing invoices using a dot matrix printer.

Multipart Paper

Definition: Paper containing multiple layers used to create duplicate copies.
Example: Printing shipping forms with carbon-copy pages.

Replace Ribbon

Definition: Installing a new ink ribbon when print quality fades.
Example: Replacing a worn dot matrix printer ribbon.

Replace Printhead

Definition: Installing a new printhead when the existing one becomes damaged.
Example: Replacing a failing printhead in an impact printer.

Replace Paper

Definition: Loading new paper into the printer.
Example: Installing a new stack of continuous-feed paper.
